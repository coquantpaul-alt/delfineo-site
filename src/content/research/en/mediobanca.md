Mediobanca occupies a unique position in European finance. Founded in 1946 by Enrico Cuccia to channel Marshall Plan capital into Italian reconstruction, it evolved into the silent architect of Italy's postwar industrial capitalism — the invisible hand behind Generali, Fiat, Pirelli, and Montedison.

Today, the bank operates three distinct engines: a CIB franchise generating €700m+ in revenues with a 30%+ RoTE, a 13% stake in Generali worth roughly €6bn at market, and a fast-growing consumer credit arm (Compass) that earns mid-teens returns on allocated capital.

The market prices Mediobanca as if the Generali stake were a deadweight conglomerate discount. We believe the opposite: the stake is a free call option on Generali's own re-rating, while the operating businesses alone justify the current share price.

Our Forward Owner Earnings framework values the operating businesses at approximately €9.5bn — implying you acquire the Generali stake for roughly 55 cents on the euro. This is a margin of safety we find compelling.
