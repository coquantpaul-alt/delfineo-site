# Electrolux reorganises global production, cuts 3,000 jobs and partners with China's Midea in North America

**The story in one line:** Electrolux will cut 3,000 jobs, raise 9 billion Swedish kronor, and hand part of its US manufacturing to a joint venture with Chinese rival Midea.

## Key numbers

- **3,000 jobs** cut over two years, about **8%** of global workforce
- **1,500 jobs** go this year, many of them in North America
- **9 billion Swedish kronor (830 million euros)** capital raise
- **1,200 staff** Midea plans to hire in 2027-2028 at the reconverted Anderson, North Carolina plant
- **Around 600 million Swedish kronor (55.5 million euros)** annual synergies from the JV by year three
- **Q3 2026** effective date of the Midea JV
- **Sites in scope:** Anderson NC (pivoting from refrigeration to laundry), a US commercial entity for refrigeration, and a Mexican refrigeration plant
- **Hungarian refrigerator plant** closed

## Why it matters

European white-goods makers have been losing ground to Chinese competitors on both cost and innovation. Letting Midea into the North American footprint — even via a JV — is a defensive move: Electrolux offloads capex and payroll while keeping brand presence. The Anderson site's pivot from refrigeration to washers is the visible sign that the production mix is being reshuffled.

The new 3,000-job plan plus the 2022 programme of 4,000 suggests the company has not yet reached steady-state. Investors will want to know whether this round finally sets a stable cost base.

## Takeaway

The structural story is the Midea JV, not the headcount cut. Electrolux is choosing partnership with a Chinese rival over head-on competition in the US — a signal the entire European appliance industry will have to consider.
