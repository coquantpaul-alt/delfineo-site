# Nike cuts 1,400 more jobs as its 'Win Now' turnaround runs into a collapsing China and geopolitical headwinds

**The story in one line:** Elliott Hill's turnaround plan keeps trimming — this time 1,400 tech-heavy roles — while Nike's China business drops 20% and the stock gives up 30% year-to-date.

## Key numbers

- **1,400 jobs** cut worldwide, or just under **2%** of total headcount
- **520 million dollars** net profit for the quarter ended February, **down 35%** year-on-year
- **11.2 billion dollars** revenue, flat
- **2% to 4%** expected decline in current-quarter sales
- **–20%** expected decline in China (Nike's third-largest market)
- **~30%** stock price decline year-to-date; **–2%** on Thursday alone
- **Previous cuts under Hill:** several hundred jobs last summer; **775** at distribution centres in January

## Why it matters

Nike has two problems at once — a demand problem in Greater China and an inventory problem in Europe and the Middle East. The Middle East war has accelerated both: it dampens consumer confidence and forces deeper promotional activity to move stock. "Win Now" is the structural response: simplify the organisation, modernise industrial tools, reorganise parts of Converse, and integrate supply-chain functions.

This round of cuts is narrower than previous ones — 1,400 is small versus Nike's total — but the fact that it hits the technology function signals where management thinks the inefficiency is.

## Takeaway

The cuts are a symptom, not the cure. What matters for the next 12 months is whether Nike can stabilise the China business and work through European inventory without further margin damage. Autumn guidance will be the next test.
