# Goldman Sachs and Morgan Stanley charged sharply different fees for access to Anthropic's $30bn private round

**The story in one line:** Two US bulge-bracket banks offered clients the same Anthropic deal with completely different fee economics — a flat 1% at Morgan Stanley, a Goldman structure closer to hedge-fund terms — creating unequal returns on identical exposure.

## Key numbers

- **350 billion dollars** valuation for Anthropic in the February 2026 round
- **30 billion dollars** raised in the round; investors included Singapore's sovereign wealth fund, Coatue and Nvidia
- **Morgan Stanley:** **1% placement fee**, no management fee, no carried interest, no fiduciary relationship
- **Goldman Sachs:** **1.25% management fee** plus **17.5% carry** on profits above an **8%** hurdle rate, structured as a single investment fund with Goldman acting as fiduciary co-investor
- **Average Goldman wealth account:** **~70 million dollars**; **Morgan Stanley:** **20 million dollars or more**
- **OpenAI retail participation:** **3 billion dollars** out of the **122 billion dollars** raised last month

## Why it matters

The Anthropic and OpenAI IPOs, plus the imminent SpaceX listing, will be among the largest stock market debuts ever. Banks are racing to build early pipelines of qualified wealth investors, and they are experimenting with how to price access to pre-IPO allocations. The Goldman structure — more hedge-fund-like than traditional placement — concentrates returns if the IPO works but lifts the break-even for investors. The Morgan Stanley structure is simpler and friendlier, but reflects the bank's role as pure distributor, not co-investor.

Regulators have historically scrutinised SPV fees when disclosure is thin. Expect the question of standardised pre-IPO access pricing to become a live theme if Anthropic or OpenAI list and returns diverge across vehicles.

## Takeaway

This is the first visible sign that the coming wave of AI IPOs is already creating unusual distribution dynamics. For investors in these SPVs, the fee structure matters as much as the deal: a successful Anthropic IPO could leave Goldman clients with materially less upside than Morgan Stanley clients in the same round.
