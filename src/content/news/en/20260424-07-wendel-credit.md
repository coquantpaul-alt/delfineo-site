# Wendel's NAV falls 3.6% as private-credit valuation pressures reach European investment firms

**The story in one line:** Wendel's Q1 NAV drop shows that the private-credit valuation correction shaking Apollo, Ares, Blackstone and Blue Owl is not just a US story.

## Key numbers

- **NAV down 3.6%** in Q1 to **158.4 euros per share**
- **–8.5 euros per share**: contribution of Monroe Capital to the NAV decline
- **~50 billion euros** assets under management at Wendel's third-party platform (via IK Partners and Monroe Capital)
- **349 million euros** in fees generated in 2025
- **3.8 billion dollars** raised by Monroe Capital in 2025
- **+129% year-on-year** Q1 fees, at **106.2 million euros**
- **US peers:** Apollo, Ares, Blackstone, BlackRock, Blue Owl all capped or managed redemptions; Blue Owl returned just over **1 billion dollars** out of **5.4 billion** requested (a 5% cap)

## Why it matters

Private credit is a 2,000 billion dollar global market. Until recently, the narrative was that Europe was less exposed than the US to the current turbulence — lower leverage, fewer retail investors. Wendel's result shows that European vehicles are not immune when market multiples applied to non-listed assets re-rate.

Operating momentum, though, is still on: Monroe raised 3.8 billion dollars in 2025, and Wendel's Q1 fee income more than doubled year-on-year. That is consistent with the idea that the hit is on the mark-to-market of holdings, not on the underlying fundraising machine.

## Takeaway

The interesting data point here is not the 3.6% NAV move — it is that European private-credit mark-downs are now visible in filings. Watch other European alternative managers' next results for confirmation of the pattern.

## Delfineo insight

Wendel illustrates a structural risk we have flagged on private-credit-heavy holding companies: NAV is only as stable as the multiples applied. Euronext-listed investment firms trading at discounts to NAV (typically 20 to 40%) usually justify the discount by liquidity and opacity — a multiples re-rating makes both worse, because the discount can widen just as the NAV shrinks. For Delfineo's European holding-company corpus (Wendel, Sofina, Tikehau Capital, GBL, Investor AB), the mark-to-market shock is the short-term risk; the fundraising momentum is the medium-term offsetting factor.
