# France pushes Greece to cede Mirage jets to Ukraine in exchange for new Rafales

**The story in one line:** Paris is brokering a three-way deal — Greek Mirage 2000-5s to Ukraine, new Dassault Rafales to Greece — but Athens is pushing back on timing and price.

## Key numbers

- **~10 Mirage 2000-5** jets Greece would cede to Ukraine under the French proposal
- **24 Mirage 2000-5** currently in the Greek fleet, plus **10** older Mirage 2000 EGM/BGM in storage
- **24 Rafales** already operational in Greece (12 used + 12 new, ordered January 2021)
- **25 billion euros** Greek defence modernisation plan through 2036; **12 billion euros** already committed
- **20 F-35s** on order from the US; **90 billion euros** EU loan potentially available to fund Ukraine's purchase of the Mirages
- **2027** when the Greek Mirage maintenance contract with Dassault expires
- **2030** original planned horizon for replacing the Mirage fleet with Rafales

## Why it matters

The negotiation sits at the intersection of three problems. First, Ukraine's appetite for combat aircraft that Europe cannot easily manufacture at scale. Second, France's fiscal constraints, which prevent direct arms transfers. Third, Greece's strategic equation with Turkey: its Mirage-plus-Rafale fleet gives it air superiority, and airspace violations by Turkey have dropped since that balance tilted. Ceding half the Mirage force before new Rafales arrive would open a capability gap the Greek public and general staff are unwilling to accept.

Beyond the jets, Macron's two-day visit is expected to renew the 2021 Franco-Greek strategic partnership on a tacit-renewal basis, and may produce contracts around Naval Group's Blacksword Barracuda submarines and KNDS's Philoctète armoured vehicles.

## Takeaway

The Mirage-Rafale swap is live but unresolved. The sticking point is money, not politics: without EU funding channelled through Kyiv, Greece cannot afford new Rafales, and Dassault will be asked to take a price hit. Watch for the language around Saturday's signed "defence contracts" — if the Mirage transfer is not mentioned, the deal has slipped to another round.

*Source: Les Echos, 24 April 2026, Basile Dekonink and Anne Bauer.*
