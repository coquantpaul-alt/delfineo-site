# The remaking of America's media order under Trump: friendly owners, aggressive lawsuits, and a more compliant FCC

**The story in one line:** In a decade, Trump has reshaped American media — aligned billionaire ownership, unprecedented defamation lawsuits, and an FCC openly framed as enforcing his preferences — while online creators eclipse the old gatekeepers.

## Key numbers

- **Fox News:** **~1.9 million daily viewers** in March; **CNN:** 641,000; **MS Now (ex-MSNBC):** 759,000
- **Trust in US media:** down to **below 30%**, from over 70% post-Watergate
- **Lawsuits filed by Trump:** **15 billion dollars** vs The New York Times; **10 billion dollars** vs Wall Street Journal; **5 billion dollars** vs BBC
- **Paramount–CBS News settlement:** **16 million dollars** (widely seen as weak case, paid to clear FCC merger approval)
- **Fox News vs Dominion:** **787.5 million dollars** defamation settlement
- **Candace Owens:** ~**6 million YouTube subscribers**, more than The New York Times
- **Key ownership shift:** David Ellison (Paramount) now controls CBS and CNN after winning Warner Bros Discovery from Netflix
- **FCC chair Brendan Carr:** warned broadcasters over Iran war coverage; Trump said he was "thrilled"

## Why it matters

The FT piece documents a structural shift, not a partisan complaint: US media ownership has concentrated among Trump-friendly figures; lawsuits and merger-review leverage have disciplined newsrooms into settlement rather than litigation (Paramount's Shari Redstone reportedly called it a "20 billion dollar suit for a company with an 8 billion dollar market cap"); and the influencer ecosystem has grown so large that Candace Owens alone has a YouTube audience bigger than The New York Times.

For businesses with exposure to US broadcasting (Paramount, Warner Bros Discovery now under Paramount, Fox, Comcast-owned MS Now), the FCC's posture on licence revocation — even if never exercised — creates a persistent chilling effect on editorial decisions, which in turn affects ad revenue mix and audience trust.

## Takeaway

Watch three gauges: next Ellison-era CBS editorial decisions, any FCC licence action, and whether the Trump-era lawsuits against NYT / WSJ / BBC actually go to trial or get settled. Settlement at scale would confirm the new playbook; a trial loss for Trump would rebalance incentives.
