# Goldman Sachs raises Brent forecast to $90 as Iran war disruption drags on

**The story in one line:** Goldman has lifted its Q4 Brent forecast to $90 — and pushed back the date the Middle East returns to normal — because the Iran war is lasting longer than the bank expected.

## Key numbers

- **$90/bbl**: Goldman's new Q4 Brent forecast, up from **$80**.
- **$83/bbl**: new Q4 WTI forecast, up from $75.
- **>$106**: Brent on Monday, **+20%** since 17 April; the early-March peak was near **$120**.
- **End of June**: revised date for Middle East export normalisation (was mid-May).
- **Up to 12mn b/d**: oil inventories cut in April due to the crisis.
- **~500,000 b/d**: long-term "scarring" of Gulf production capacity, mostly in Iraq.
- **~$84.80**: December Brent futures — markets still expect prices to fall later.

## Why it matters

Two signals matter for investors here. First, longer-dated futures still anticipate a return to the $80s, which means the spot curve is in **backwardation** — near-term prices above longer-term ones, a classic stress signal that also pays current holders to keep oil. Second, Goldman flags the risk of US oil export restrictions: a politically tempting but disruptive move that would widen the Brent-WTI gap and squeeze refiners outside the United States. The S&P 500 and Nasdaq Composite hitting record highs suggests equities are not yet pricing the scenario.

## Takeaway

The base case has shifted from "spike then normalise by mid-May" to "elevated through Q3 with structural Iraqi damage". Watch the Strait of Hormuz reopening date and any move by Washington on export controls — both will define how the second half plays out.
