Aleph Alpha shareholders will receive one Cohere share for every nine shares held, according to two sources familiar with the terms; Cohere will retain its name and operate from dual headquarters in Canada and Germany.

  The deal reflects a broader pushback against reliance on Silicon Valley: it follows SpaceX's acquisition of coding start-up Cursor and is an early sign of consolidation in the AI sector. Cohere was founded in 2019 in Toronto by former Google researchers, focuses on enterprise large language models deployed within clients' own infrastructure, and was valued at 6.8 billion dollars last year. Heidelberg-based Aleph Alpha was valued at roughly 500 million euros in a 2023 round and has struggled to keep up with larger rivals; its former CEO Jonas Andrulis stepped down earlier this year to found a new AI start-up with consultancy Roland Berger.

  The merger comes with a fresh funding round led by Schwarz Digits, the tech arm of German retailer Schwarz Group (owner of Lidl supermarkets), which has committed 600 million dollars in equity and research funding. Schwarz is already the biggest backer of Aleph Alpha (above 20% stake) and is building an 11 billion-euro data centre near Berlin to support AI workloads; those data centres will host Cohere's AI systems. The German government will act as anchor customer for sovereign, secure AI in public procurement. Canada's AI minister Evan Solomon told the FT that "middle powers" need "an option between the hyperscalers and the hegemon". # Cohere and Aleph Alpha agree $20bn transatlantic AI tie-up, backed by German and Canadian governments

**The story in one line:** Cohere takes over Aleph Alpha in a 20 billion dollar deal, with Berlin and Ottawa explicitly positioning the combined group as a European-Canadian alternative to OpenAI and Google.

## Key numbers

- **20 billion dollars** combined company value
- **Cohere 2024 valuation:** **6.8 billion dollars**
- **Aleph Alpha 2023 valuation:** **~500 million euros**
- **Share ratio:** **1 Cohere share for every 9 Aleph Alpha shares**
- **Schwarz Digits commitment:** **600 million dollars** in equity and research funding (already >20% stake in Aleph Alpha)
- **Schwarz data centre near Berlin:** **11 billion euros**, to host Cohere AI workloads
- **Geography:** dual HQ in Canada and Germany; German government as anchor customer for public procurement

## Why it matters

Both Cohere and Aleph Alpha have focused on enterprise and government customers rather than consumers, betting on a market for AI that is deployed inside client infrastructure rather than called via a US hyperscaler API. That market was niche a year ago; the deal signals Berlin and Ottawa now see it as strategically necessary. The Schwarz Group involvement is the industrial anchor — retail data centres are already at continental scale, and supplying compute to a sovereign AI provider converts Lidl's parent company into a European hyperscaler counterweight.

The deal also confirms that AI sector consolidation is starting at the mid-tier: Cohere + Aleph Alpha is the first major cross-border merger aimed at critical mass in the enterprise segment. Expect similar moves in France (Mistral) and the UK (Stability, DeepMind carve-outs).

## Takeaway

For European investors, the interesting signal is the Schwarz Group's role: an under-the-radar industrial player with the data-centre capacity to anchor a non-US AI stack. Whether the combined Cohere-Aleph Alpha can actually close the capability gap with OpenAI and Anthropic at the model level is the harder question — and the 20 billion dollar valuation prices in considerable optimism.
