# Meta to cut 10% of jobs to 'offset' Mark Zuckerberg's AI spending spree

**The story in one line:** Meta cuts 10% of staff — roughly 8,000 jobs — on 20 May to fund a capex near-doubling to 135 billion dollars and Zuckerberg's bet on "personal superintelligence".

## Key numbers

- **10% of staff**: approximately **8,000 jobs** cut, effective **20 May**
- **6,000 more**: open positions Meta is no longer filling
- **18 months**: healthcare coverage in the severance package for US employees
- **1.7 trillion dollars**: Meta's market cap
- **135 billion dollars**: 2026 capex guidance (nearly double 2025), per January disclosure
- **Muse Spark**: new Meta AI model rolled out this month; Meta acknowledges it lags rivals
- **Meta Compute**: data-centre programme targeting **tens of gigawatts this decade**, then hundreds; each gigawatt costs **tens of billions of dollars**
- **Microsoft parallel**: offered voluntary redundancy to ~7% of US staff the same day

## Why it matters

Meta's lay-offs and Microsoft's same-day voluntary-departure programme together mark the clearest signal yet that Big Tech is willing to trade headcount for GPU capacity. The strategic logic is that marginal dollars return more in AI infrastructure than in incremental software engineers, particularly when a fraction of engineering work is being automated in-house.

The tracking-software plan — recording mouse movements, clicks, keystrokes and screen content to train agent models — is the more uncomfortable side of the trade. Employees fear they are labelling the training data for the systems that will make their own roles redundant. Whether that affects talent retention at Meta will matter for the next 12 months.

## Takeaway

The Meta cuts are the second leg (after Microsoft) of a Big Tech cost rotation toward AI capex. Investors should read this as Zuckerberg reaffirming his AI commitment even as the share price wobbles on spending concerns. The Muse Spark admission — Meta acknowledges it lags OpenAI and Google — is the key operational risk: the capex must buy a model that closes that gap.
