# Leading central banks play for time on interest rate rises

**The story in one line:** the Fed, ECB, BoJ, BoE and Bank of Canada are all expected to hold rates this week as the Iran war and oil-price volatility cloud their inflation forecasts.

## Key numbers

- **3.5–3.75%**: Fed funds target range, expected to be held on Wednesday.
- **2.8%**: US core PCE inflation in February, still above the 2% goal.
- **2%**: ECB policy rate; markets price **two more hikes this year**.
- **~0.75%**: BoJ rate; an April hike is now seen as very unlikely.
- **3.75%**: BoE rate; traders have backed away from near-term increases.
- **2nd energy shock in 5 years** — the parallel rate-setters most fear is 2021-22.

## Why it matters

Central banks are stuck between two errors. Move too late and inflation expectations re-anchor higher, as Fed governor Chris Waller has warned. Move too early and they tighten into a war-driven supply shock that could pass. Policymakers are leaning on scenario analysis instead of central forecasts, partly because Donald Trump's posts on the conflict are themselves moving energy markets in real time. ECB chief economist Philip Lane says the bank cannot judge the shock before June at the earliest.

## Takeaway

Expect a synchronised hold this week. The next big inflection point is data, not rhetoric: rate-setters need at least a full month of post-war inflation prints — and ideally signs that the Strait of Hormuz is reopening — before they will commit either way.
