# SAP launches sovereign cloud offering in France with S3ns as 'tech becomes geopolitical'

**The story in one line:** SAP is partnering with S3ns, the Thales–Google Cloud joint venture, to sell a French "sovereign" cloud offering, betting that regulation and the US Cloud Act will accelerate European cloud migration.

## Key numbers

- **SecNumCloud (December 2025)**: S3ns's ANSSI certification, France's "trusted cloud" label for sensitive data.
- **End of 2026**: commercial launch date of the SAP–S3ns offering.
- **"Customer zero"**: Thales, deploying it for defence and cyber-security.
- **#2 European tech group** by market cap: SAP's standing.
- **27 separate clouds**: the EU status quo SAP attacks — its argument for a "digital union".

## Why it matters

The offering rests on a compromise: S3ns gives French clients Google Cloud's technology while satisfying France's data-protection rules. That is exactly the grey zone the US Cloud Act makes politically explosive. Pure-play French rivals and some cyber-security experts argue that a majority-French joint venture plugged into a US technology stack does not fully shield data from a US extraterritorial request. By stacking partnerships with Mistral, cloud Bleu and now S3ns, SAP is hedging across every credible local option — its real fear is letting AWS and Microsoft capture European migrations alone.

## Takeaway

This is more than a tech announcement. It is a real-time test of how fast regulated French heavyweights move to cloud — and which model, "certified sovereign" or "global hyperscaler", wins their most sensitive workloads.
