**The story in one line.** Paris opened MBDA and Safran's gates to EU Defence Commissioner Andrius Kubilius to make sure the €60bn of European loans to Ukraine is channelled to European makers rather than the United States.

## Key numbers

- **€60bn:** European loans being unlocked for Ukraine's defence.
- **2,000 missiles** have struck Ukraine, of which **900 ballistic**; **2 to 3 anti-ballistic missiles** are needed to destroy one.
- **US Patriot production:** capped at **750/year**, being redirected to US stockpiles by the Iran war.
- **Safran AASM output:** **×4 in four years**, to **1,200 units in 2025**; France-Norway order for Kyiv of **more than NOK 7bn**.
- **MBDA Meteor missile:** more than **5,000 km/h**.
- **SAMP/T NG (MBDA-Thales):** **150 km** intercept range, **40,000 components** (**60%** from subcontractors); Ukraine test in 2026.
- **Aster (SAMP/T missile component):** output **×5** between 2024 and 2025, **×2** in 2026; lead time **18 months** vs. three years in 2022.
- **MBDA investment:** **€5bn** over the coming years.
- **Raytheon-Germany Patriot contract for Kyiv:** **€3.7bn**.

## Why it matters

An inertial navigation system is a device that computes a missile's or aircraft's position using only internal sensors (accelerometers, gyroscopes), without GPS or external signals. That is Safran's competitive edge: US Patriot systems depend on GPS, which is jammable, while AASM can strike in GPS-contested environments. An industrial "payment-in-kind" is at work: demand for anti-ballistic missiles is blowing out (2-3 per intercept × 900 Russian ballistic missiles = structural deficit vs. 750 Patriot per year), and the Iran war is pulling American production back into US stockpiles.

The political risk for Paris is the EU's SAFE programme: it permits derogations for buying non-European kit, and several German players — Rheinmetall with Lockheed Martin on the GMARS rocket launcher, Diehl Defence with Raytheon on a surface-to-air missile — have struck transatlantic deals. Without a credible European offer, the €60bn would de facto fund US industrials.

## Takeaway

France is running the strategic-autonomy score on both technology (Safran's GPS-free inertial) and cadence (×5 then ×2 on Aster). The 2026 in-theatre test of the SAMP/T NG in Ukraine will be the full-scale validation — a success clarifies the choice vs. Patriot; a failure reinforces US buyers.
