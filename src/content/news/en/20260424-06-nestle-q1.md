# Nestlé beats Q1 expectations on coffee, culinary and snacks volumes and keeps 2026 targets despite infant-milk crisis

**The story in one line:** Volumes are back — up 1.2% vs a 0.2% consensus — and the stock rallied 6%, even as the group's infant-milk recall and Middle East exposure cloud the outlook.

## Key numbers

- **21.3 billion Swiss francs (23.1 billion euros)** Q1 revenue, **–5.7%** year-on-year
- **Consensus:** 21.2 billion Swiss francs (revenue beat by a narrow margin)
- **+1.2%** volume growth vs **+0.2%** consensus forecast
- **+6.20%** share-price move to 80.22 Swiss francs (87.4 euros); SMI index **+1.04%**
- **2026 guidance reaffirmed:** organic sales growth around **3% to 4%**, accelerating volumes vs 2025
- **~3%** of group revenue exposed to the Middle East
- **180 positions** at risk in Nestlé France from 2027, with net impact estimated at **75 to 100**

## Why it matters

The thesis on Nestlé had been: post-inflation consumers shifted to supermarket private labels, volumes were stuck, and the new boss Philipp Navratil had to show that price cuts and product adjustments would bring shoppers back. Q1 says yes — at least for coffee, culinary, and snacks. Volume growth six times the consensus is a strong first data point on Navratil's plan.

Two overhangs remain. First, the infant-milk scandal is still pressing on organic growth and brand trust. Second, Middle East exposure is manageable in revenue terms (3%) but unpredictable on the cost side: commodity and distribution costs could blow up margins.

## Takeaway

Nestlé's Q1 is the first piece of evidence that the volume recovery is real. Whether it lasts depends on whether the commodity cost picture stays contained. The market voted yes for now; the full-year call is more nuanced.
