# MPS chief considers selling €7.4bn Generali stake to fund a Banco BPM takeover

**The story in one line:** MPS may sell the 13% Generali stake it inherited from Mediobanca to fund a bid for Banco BPM, reviving Rome's long-standing plan to forge a national banking champion — though MPS publicly denies the sale is under review.

## Key numbers

- **7.4 billion euros**: value of the 13% Generali stake held by MPS (via Mediobanca, acquired last year)
- **~9%**: UniCredit CEO Andrea Orcel's separate stake in Generali
- **UniCredit's earlier bid for Banco BPM**: blocked by Rome; Orcel pivoted to Germany's Commerzbank
- **Crédit Agricole stake in Banco BPM**: raised to **20%** during the earlier UniCredit episode
- **Preferred stake buyers named**: Intesa Sanpaolo, UniCredit — both structurally constrained
- **MPS board composition**: Lovaglio reinstated last week after being ousted a month earlier over disagreements including the Generali stake

## Why it matters

Italy's government has wanted a "third pole" of banking alongside Intesa and UniCredit for nearly two years — and MPS-Banco BPM is the version it keeps coming back to. The Generali stake sale is the funding mechanism, but the political constraint is that Generali is viewed as a strategic backstop to Italian government debt. So the stake cannot be sold to just anyone: Rome wants it in "safe hands", which narrows the buyer pool and complicates pricing.

That MPS publicly denied the sale is being examined, after five sources described the discussions to the FT, is consistent with how such transactions typically progress — through leaks, denials and then formal announcements. The more important signal is Lovaglio's reinstatement: the board that ousted him over the Generali stake lost, which implies alignment on a strategy that involves deploying or monetising the stake.

## Takeaway

Watch for two signals in coming weeks: any shareholder dividend discussion from MPS (the alternative structure to an outright sale), and any fresh move by UniCredit on Generali now that Orcel sits just under 9%. Orcel's parallel play is the wildcard.
